## ❓ What is VRAMDISK
VRAMDISK creates a hard drive out of your computer's RAM. RAM works much faster than standard hard drives or solid-state drives. By using a portion of your RAM as a storage space, you can load files and run applications at faster rates than usual. This process keeps your data in your system memory. Accessing this data remains immediate because the computer does not need to read from the disk drive.

The software sets up this virtual volume on your Windows system. Once you create the disk, it appears in your File Explorer as a new drive letter. You move or copy files into this drive just like any other folder. You gain speed benefits for tasks that require high-speed data access.

## ⚠️ Important Considerations
Because a VRAM disk uses your computer's RAM, the data stored on it disappears when you turn off or restart your computer. RAM requires constant power to hold information. 

You should always copy your files back to your permanent hard drive before you close your computer. Do not leave files in the VRAM disk if you need them after a restart. You can use the "Backup" feature within the software menu to save the contents of the disk to your main hard drive automatically. 

VRAMDISK includes a feature to restore your disk contents on startup if you trigger this option in the settings. Check the box labeled "Save image on shutdown" to keep your files persistent across sessions.
